I Think

Not really a smile, but not really a frown. The dog just looked at me through the bars, curiosity in his eyes.

“I do the same thing. It ain’t fun. Thinking about what I should be doing. Or what she said. Or what I gotta do tomorrow. Or what should’ve been done yesterday. What’s on your mind, shaggy dog?”

“I think it’s you who's got something on their mind.”

“It’s a mess really. I worry I won't do anything of importance.”

“You just sit around.”

“And talk.”

“Is it a waste now then? Cause we aren’t doing anything?”

“Sometimes…I feel like it is.”

For once, I almost wanted to talk for hours. It was like speaking in a mirror.
