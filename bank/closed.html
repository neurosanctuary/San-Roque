Closed

“Sorry, we’re closed.”

His tone was soft and slow.

“Your sign said you were open.”

“Ah…well, there’s been a robbery.”

“This place looks like it hasn’t been touched in years.”

“I meant…we’re…uh…out of money.”

“I don’t need money.”

With each word he sunk further and further into his seat. Was it my voice? My appearance? The way I opened the door? Oh man, did I slam the door? Or maybe…I walked in too wickedly or…perhaps the way I stood out the counter gave off a threatening presence…did I have something in my teeth? Did my—

“Um…if you’re looking for our newly installed post office, it’s to your left. You can help yourself.”

“No, I need your help on something.”

“I’m not really a good mailman. They just kinda dumped it on me when the old one burnt down.”

“Not mail related, I just need you to look—”

“I’m not really a good bank teller.”

“Can you just look at this photo? Have you seen the dog in it?”

Please don’t say anything more than a yes or no answer. Please.
