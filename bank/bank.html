Bank

An incredibly small building for an incredibly small town, though I couldn’t have expected much from a place like this. The wood that lines its walls looks infested with termites, and any press on it could knock the whole thing down. One of the windows is shattered, a few spider webs covering the spiked insides.

It’s more of a shack than a bank, but a sign doesn’t lie.

The inside is just as you would expect it to look; dusty, worn-down, and unused.

An overflowing box of letters stands on the side of the teller’s booth, my eyes gaze above through the bars where the teller is staring at me.

A Xoloitzcuintli.

Eyes-closed.

Tense as one could be.

Mumbling to himself.

“Hello there.”
